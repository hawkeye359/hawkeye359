A big step towards gender equality. SC orders centre to allow women in NDA

Saying 'gender equality issues cannot be postponed', the Supreme Court said women candidates must be allowed to appear for the November 2021 National Defence Academy exam.

Manoj Kumar

24/08/2021

The Supreme Court on August 18 ruled that women be allowed to face the National Defence Academy (NDA) entrance exam for gaining commission as officers in defence services. With this, not only have the doors been thrown open for women to gain permanent commission in the Indian Army, Air Force and Navy, the decision has long-term ramifications regarding the future of women in the armed forces.

At present, women are recruited in the defence services as short service commission officers. On March 17, 2020, the Supreme Court ordered women officers to be given the same option of converting their short service commission to permanent commission—as given to male officers.

The first batch of women officers was given commission in the Navy in 1992. It has taken close to 30 years for women to be given direct permanent commission. Commander Prasanna Edayilliam (retired), the first woman officer from the Indian Navy to challenge the denial of permanent commission to women officers in court in 2010, welcomed the decision. “If a woman clears the NDA written test, the physical test and outdoor training imparted at the NDA, she ought to be treated at par with the men who accomplish the same,” she said. “The idea is to groom someone from a young age. A young male is not born an admiral. If the same opportunities are given to women, I suppose in 30 years from now, India could have its first woman Navy chief.”

To understand what gaining entry into the defence services through the NDA means, a look at the various options for entry would be pertinent. The NDA recruits personnel right after leaving school. Another mode of entry is through the Combined Defence Services (CDS) exam, which is meant for graduates. Commissions through both channels are permanent. Besides, there is a permanent commission for those who have done their post-graduation, which is usually for engineers, electronics and mechanical engineers (EMEs), judge and advocate general (JAGs), signals, army educational corps (AECs), etc. The Officer Training Academy (OTA) in Chennai and Gaya recruits short service commission officers, both men and women. This is presently the only entry option available for women.

While recruits and trainees of all academies are at par, on paper, once they have passed out, it is the NDA near Pune that is considered the elite institution among them. At present, the top positions in the army, navy and air force are usually manned by NDA recruits. The reason is simple. NDA recruits have an advantage as their entry age is between 16.5 and 19. In defence services, where retirement age is tied to the rank held, the youngest in a batch have a very clear benefit.

While the nuts and bolts of recruitment of women officers through the NDA is to be worked out, what is clear is that women will get permanent commission and, as a result, commission in what are called ‘Arms’ or the main fighting unit. Promotions in the army also depend on the formation the officer hails from. In the case of the army, being from the infantry, artillery or armoured corps holds an advantage in promotions as there are more vacancies within the formation for officers to rise up the chain. Also, top positions of corps commanders or army commanders, and finally the chief, are almost always held by those from the ‘Arms’. Gaining entry into the fighting arms will put women in the reckoning for all top positions in the army. “That is a possibility in the future. Once the commission is permanent, all roles are open for women,” says Vice Admiral Suresh Bangara (retired), a former commandant of the NDA.

How will the NDA prepare itself for this historic decision? “The Supreme Court saw the issue from the point of equality. Adjustments will have to be made in physical infrastructure for women when they arrive at the NDA. Already, with enhanced induction levels, the number of squadrons of trainees has been increased,” says Bangara.

The three service chiefs visited the NDA on August 20 to take stock of the situation and prepare the academy for the arrival of women as trainees on its premises.

Even in the west, where women officers in the navy have been given command of battleships, the new ships being built are designed in a way that they have women-specific amenities. “Some modifications may need to be done. But if women have gone to space and conquered Mount Everest, these changes can be easily made to facilitate them,” said Prasanna. It is not clear yet how many women will make it to the NDA after the written test and other formalities to be held November onwards. “Most importantly, we do not want any reservation for entry. We want the same training, the same merit as men. There can then be no denial of higher commands for women,” she added.

HOW TO CRACK AIRFORCE GROUP X AND Y EXAM IN 45 DAYS (TRICKS & TIPS)

Manoj Kumar

24/08/2021

Central Airmen Selection Board (CASB), Indian Air Force (IAF) has released the tentative exam date for the Air Force Group X & Y Posts on its official website https://airmenselection.cdac.in, now it will be held in first week of November.

It is a very big question in front of a student who is preparing for Air Force X and Y Group that How to prepare for the Air Force Group X and Y exam in 45 days? Nowadays, this exam is very popular among the students who have cleared 10+2, as it offers the gateway to joining the Air Force at a very early age.

Seeing the very less time remaining for the exam, one has to make a proper strategy for the online written exam first.

Written Test is the first stage of the selection procedure of Air Force Group X and Y, which is the most important and tough hurdle for the candidates. Most of the elimination is done at this stage.

Air Force “X” exam consists of English, Physics, and Maths of 10+2 level and the time duration is of 60 minutes.

Similarly, Group “Y” consists of English, Reasoning and General Awareness and the time duration will be 45 minutes.

Candidates opting for both groups “X” and “Y” will have to give papers of English, Physics, Maths, Reasoning and General Awareness and the time duration will be 85 minutes.

STRATEGY REQUIRED FOR CRACKING THE EXAM IN 45 DAYS:

Air Force Groups X and Y exam consist mainly of three stages out of which the first two stages are very difficult and need proper planning and preparation. Now here we will discuss the complete strategy to clear Air Force Group “X” and “Y” Exam.

Let’s now discuss the preparation of each subject:

English:

English is included in both Groups “X” and “Y”. The question generally comes from topics like Comprehension, Synonym/Antonym, Error detection, Jumbled Sentence, Active/Passive Voice, and Tense.

Strategy:

English subject plays a vital role in every competitive exam, but seeing the time constraint we have to focus on the short time strategy. Make a habit of reading English newspaper every day, write difficult words and learn them regularly, which will help you to improve your vocabulary. This practice will help you in a one-word situation, Synonyms, and Antonyms.

Attempting mock test papers and quizzes of past exams would also assist you in scoring good marks.

Physics:

The Physics subject is only applicable to the candidates who are appearing for Air Force Group “X” Exam. Syllabus covers all the topic of class 11th and 12th. Questions will be of both types- Theoretical and Numerical. One should have a better understanding of concepts for application knowledge.
